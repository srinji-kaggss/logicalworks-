# Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective date:** when first customer signs.

This policy exists so that prospective customers know, before they invest time in an evaluation, whether Logical Works is the right partner for their business. It is not a legal document. It is a statement of operational boundaries.

---

## 1. Who we serve

Logical Works exists for operators who run businesses where trust is the product:

- **Financial advisors** with fiduciary duties to their clients.
- **Legal professionals** bound by professional-conduct rules.
- **Agencies** that manage sensitive data for end clients.
- **Independent contractors** whose reputation is their primary asset.

If your business model depends on trust, legibility, and auditability, we are built for you.

---

## 2. Who we will not serve

We decline business with the following categories. This list is specific, not generic. We do not use phrases like "harmful businesses." We name the thing.

| Category | Examples (non-exhaustive) |
|---|---|
| **Payday lending and high-interest consumer lending** | Any operation whose primary revenue is from short-term loans with APRs above 36% to consumers. |
| **Multi-level marketing (MLM)** | Recruitment-based "business opportunities" where revenue is primarily from downstream participant fees rather than end-product sales. |
| **Surveillance-as-a-service** | Location tracking, employee monitoring, or covert data collection sold as the primary product. |
| **Regulatory arbitrage of consumer-protection law** | Operations structured primarily to evade licensing, disclosure, or duty-of-care requirements. |
| **Businesses under active regulatory enforcement for consumer harm** | Any entity currently subject to a consent order, cease-and-desist, or similar enforcement for materially harming consumers. |

**Why this matters:** Refusing revenue is the only credible trust signal a small SaaS can produce. If we would serve anyone who pays, our security claims are indistinguishable from marketing.

---

## 3. What we will not help with on-platform

The Logical Works AI assistance layer will refuse the following categories of request regardless of customer identity:

- **Generating high-volume cold-outreach content** designed to evade anti-spam law (CAN-SPAM, CASL, GDPR).
- **Producing communications designed to obscure material commercial terms** — e.g., burying fee structures, omitting risk disclosures, or mimicking official correspondence.
- **Automating debt-collection harassment** or other conduct prohibited by the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (or Canadian equivalent).
- **Generating fake reviews, testimonials, or social proof** for products or services.
- **Creating deepfakes or synthetic media** intended to deceive a counterparty.

These refusals are enforced at the intent-classifier layer and logged. They are not post-hoc content-moderation decisions.

---

## 4. Enforcement

If we determine that a customer is in violation of this policy:

1. **Notice.** We provide a written notice (email to the account owner) with a description of the violation and the specific policy section.
2. **Response period.** The customer has **30 calendar days** to remedy the violation or demonstrate that the determination was incorrect.
3. **Export.** During the response period, the customer may export all data via the standard API and UI. We do not charge for export bandwidth.
4. **Refund.** If we terminate the relationship, we refund any unused prepaid time on a pro-rata basis.
5. **No silent terminations.** We do not delete accounts without notice. We do not lock data without a remediation path.

---

## 5. Reporting misuse

If you believe a Logical Works customer is using the platform in violation of this policy:

- **Email:** `abuse@logicalworks.co` (monitored by the Security team, not general support).
- **Response timeline:** We acknowledge receipt within **2 business days** and provide a substantive response within **10 business days**.
- **Confidentiality:** Reports may be submitted anonymously. We do not disclose the reporter's identity to the subject without legal compulsion.

---

## 6. Refusals log

We maintain a public, anonymised log of every customer we have declined or terminated under this policy. No names. Dates, categories, and one-line reasons only. See [`governance/aup-refusals.md`](aup-refusals.md).

The existence of the log is the point. A policy without evidence of enforcement is indistinguishable from a press release.
